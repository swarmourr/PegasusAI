# Introducing Pegasus AI

**PegasusAI** is the next-generation AI-driven scientific workflow management system developed by a multi-institutional team funded by the **U.S. National Science Foundation** (Award #2513101).

## What Problem Does It Solve?

Modern scientific workflows — from genomics pipelines to climate simulations — involve thousands of interdependent tasks running across heterogeneous computing resources. Managing these workflows manually is error-prone, inefficient, and doesn't scale.

Pegasus AI addresses this by embedding artificial intelligence at every stage of the workflow lifecycle:

- **Create** — AI suggests related workflows and checks for composition errors
- **Plan** — ML models map tasks to optimal resources
- **Provision** — Anomaly detection flags misbehaving resources
- **Execute** — Real-time monitoring with adaptive replanning
- **Publish** — Automated provenance capture for reproducibility

## Why It Matters

Scientific computing is entering a new era. Experiments that once required dedicated HPC clusters can now run across distributed, cloud-hybrid environments. But this flexibility comes at a cost: complexity. Pegasus AI is designed to absorb that complexity so scientists can focus on their research.

## Who Is Behind It?

Pegasus AI is a collaboration between the **USC Information Sciences Institute**, **University of Southern California**, and partner institutions. The project builds on over 20 years of Pegasus WMS development.
